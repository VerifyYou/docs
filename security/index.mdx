## Privacy by design

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Your systems get a verdict, never a face" icon="shield-check" iconType="light">
    When a check finishes, you receive a verdict and a token. The API does not return a photo, face image, name, or ID document, so your systems have no reason to hold any of them.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The selfie becomes a code, not an image" icon="image-slash" iconType="light">
    The camera runs on our hosted check, not your product. We do not store images. What recognizes a returning person is a face vector, a numerical representation of the face's geometry, not a photo, treated as sensitive biometric data.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Not KYC, by choice" icon="id-badge" iconType="light">
    We do not need to know who someone is, only that they are a real, unique human. So you carry far less personal data than a government-ID check would leave you holding, and none of the documents or ID images that come with it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How the check works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start the check">
    From the browser with `vycheck()`, or from your server with `POST /v3/initialize`, which returns the hosted check URL you send the person to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The person verifies">
    On our hosted page, a short check confirms a live, present human. A short, active check is what resists injected video, replays, and deepfakes. A passive photo check cannot tell a live person from a recording.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the verdict server side">
    The person returns with `vyt` and `vyc` on the URL, where `vyc` is only a UI hint. Your server exchanges the token at `GET /v3/confirmations/{token}` for the authoritative verdict, and you gate on that. The face is never sent to your systems; on our hosted check it becomes a one-way face vector, and no raw photo is stored.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What we keep

We keep what it takes to return a verdict and to recognize a unique human, and not more. Retention differs by data type and plan, and is configurable for enterprise.

</Note>

## Keys and result integrity

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Two key types" icon="key" iconType="light">
    Publishable keys (`pk_`) are safe in the browser and only start a check. Secret keys (`sk_`) stay on your server and read verdicts. Each comes in test and live; test runs a sandbox flow that creates test users.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Signed, encrypted delivery" icon="lock-keyhole" iconType="light">
    All traffic runs over HTTPS with TLS. Webhooks are signed with a secret separate from your API key, so verify the signature with a constant time compare and make your handler idempotent.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Access and overrides

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Named seats" icon="users" iconType="light">
    Every teammate has their own login, with no shared accounts, so dashboard actions map to a real person.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Human only overrides" icon="user-shield" iconType="light">
    A real person who is wrongly denied can be cleared by a human, scoped to your own account. There is no automatic admit on a failed check.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Shared responsibility

<Columns cols={2}>
  <div>
    **We take care of**
    - Running and securing the hosted check and our API
    - The liveness and uniqueness model, kept current as attacks change
    - Protecting the face vector and verification record on our infrastructure
    - Signing webhooks and securing data in transit
  </div>
  <div>
    **You take care of**
    - Keeping secret keys server side, out of client code
    - Verifying webhook signatures and handling the verdict correctly
    - Deciding what access a verified or failed result grants
    - Anything your own systems collect outside the check
  </div>
</Columns>
