A HumanCheck is a quick face scan that proves a real, unique, live human is on the other end. But to the person taking it, the check is not a stranger asking. It is you. Every check carries your business identity, so the flow introduces itself in your name and shows your logo, or a monogram of your name if you have not set one.

Here is what they see, and each part you control.

## What the person verifying sees

When someone opens your check, the flow greets them with your name: "Verifying you for [your company name]". Your logo, or a clean monogram of your company name if you have not set one, appears with it. If you have written a pre-check message, they read that before the scan begins. When they finish, they land back with you, on the page the check was pointed at when it was created. And if they decline or are turned away, the message they see can be in your words too.

To the person on the other end, it reads as your brand doing the asking, with VerifyYou quietly doing the checking. That identity is made of four parts. The first two are the essentials; the last two shape the wording at the edges of the flow.

<Note>
  Where a check sends people, its redirect URL, is set on the verification itself when you create it, not here in branding. The [quickstart](/learn/quickstart) shows how, and if you are gating with code, it is the page where your integration reads the result.
</Note>

## Company name

The name shown throughout the verification experience.

- Every live check carries one. You can set it when you create the check, or our team sets it with you during setup.
- It fills in the greeting the person sees: "Verifying you for [company name]".
- Change it, and what people see changes with it. The check stays the same; only the name on the door is different.

Use the name your users already know. If your product name and your legal entity differ, pick the one a person would expect to see at your signup or survey.

## Logo

The logo shown in the verification experience.

- **Optional.** If you have not set one, the check shows a monogram instead: the first letter of your company name. There is always a sensible default, so a check never looks unfinished.
- Give us your logo and it replaces the monogram everywhere the person sees it. Your VerifyYou contact applies the change for you.

A logo makes the check feel like yours. It is worth setting even though the monogram covers you.

## The edges of the flow

Two more controls are about wording. Both let you speak to the person at the moments that matter most: just before the scan, and on the way out if things do not go their way.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pre-check message" icon="message" iconType="light">
    A toggle plus a short custom message, shown before the check begins. It is character-limited on purpose: a sentence or two to prime or reassure. A good one says why you are asking and how quick it will be.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Decline banner" icon="hand" iconType="light">
    A toggle plus your own copy, shown when a person declines the check or is turned away. You set the wording, so the exit reads in your voice rather than a generic one.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  The decline banner pairs naturally with the allow decline setting, which gives people a graceful way out of the check. If you offer the exit, the banner is your chance to say what happens next: who to contact, or what they can still do without verifying. See [configuring your verification](/learn/configuring-your-verification) for how decline works.
</Tip>

## Setting it up and changing it later

Your branding is part of setup. When our team creates your workspace, we set the company name and logo with you, so your check arrives already carrying your brand. [Getting access](/learn/getting-access) covers what to have ready; the short version is your company name and a logo if you have one handy.

Want to change something later? A new logo, fresh decline copy, a different pre-check message: tell your VerifyYou contact and we will update it for you. The change applies to your active check without rebuilding anything, so a check that is already live simply starts showing the new branding.

<Note>
  None of this changes what your systems receive. Whatever branding a check carries, your business still gets back a verdict, never a face, a name, or an image. See [what we store](/learn/what-we-store) for the full picture.
</Note>

## About VerifyYou

VerifyYou is the company behind HumanCheck, a camera-based check that proves a real, unique, live human is present, without documents, government ID, or the cost and friction of a full KYC check. Companies put HumanCheck in front of signups, surveys, payouts, and gated offers to keep bots and duplicate people out while letting genuine people through with a quick face scan. The scan becomes a face vector, a numerical representation rather than a saved image, and the business receives a verdict, never a face. We think of it as the trust layer between nothing and KYC. You can read more in [what a human check is](/learn/what-humancheck-is).
