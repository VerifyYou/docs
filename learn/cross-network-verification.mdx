---

Every [HumanCheck](/learn/why-humancheck), the quick camera check that proves someone is a real, unique human, runs on one shared network. When a person passes a check on any platform that uses HumanCheck, the network keeps their verified record (built on a face vector, a numerical representation of the face's geometry, never a saved image), so when the next platform recognizes them, by their device or their verified contact details, it already knows they are real and unique. That is what cross-network verification means: a person verified anywhere on VerifyYou can be recognized on your platform without scanning again. It is the least friction for them, and you stay in full control of the bar.

## Verified once, recognized everywhere

The lightest path is there for a real person: if they verified with HumanCheck on another platform in the network, your check can let them through without making them repeat the scan. Your platform still gets the same verdict: a real, unique human.

How dependably a returning person is recognized comes down to what kind of record they have. A **guest** is recognized by their device ID and nothing else, no phone, no email, no name, and guest records expire over time. A **member** has a verified phone number or email bound to their face record, proven with a one-time code during the check, so recognition follows them across devices and browsers. If repeat visitors matter on your platform, members are the reliable path.

## You set your own rules

Cross-network recognition never lowers your bar, because your check decides how much to ask of a returning person. The setting behind this is called skip re-verify: in plain language, whether someone who has already proven they are a real, unique human walks straight through, scans again after a while, or scans every single time. Your check arrives configured for your use case, and your VerifyYou contact tunes it with you as your needs change.

Your rules are yours alone. A stricter setting on your platform does not change what any other platform does, and theirs does not change yours.

## Uniqueness across the network

The network works in the other direction too. HumanCheck's uniqueness check catches a collision, the same face turning up where your rules say it should not, and you choose how wide that net reaches. That choice is called collision scope. With **network** scope, a new face is checked against everyone verified anywhere on VerifyYou. With **company** scope, it is checked only against your own verifications, so what a person does elsewhere never counts against them with you. Either way, you also choose what a collision does: block the check outright, or let it through flagged as suspicious so your team can review it.

## Why a network is worth more than a list

On your own you can only recognize the people you have already seen. On the network, a person verified elsewhere can be known to you on day one, and the value compounds as more platforms join.
