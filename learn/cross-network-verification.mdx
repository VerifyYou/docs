---
title: "Cross-network verification"
---

[HumanCheck](/learn/why-humancheck) is one network, not one platform. A person who has already verified with HumanCheck somewhere else can be recognized as a real, unique human on your platform without scanning again. That is the least friction for them, and you stay in full control of the bar.

## Verified once, recognized everywhere

The default is the lightest path for a real person. If they verified with HumanCheck on another platform in the network, we can let them through on yours without making them repeat the scan. Your platform still gets the same verdict: a real, unique human.

## You set your own rules

Cross-network recognition never lowers your bar, because you decide how much to ask for. Turn the friction up whenever your use case needs it.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Frictionless" icon="bolt" iconType="light">
    Recognize a returning or network-verified person with no scan at all. Best for low-stakes flows where completion matters most.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Re-verify on a cadence" icon="clock-rotate-left" iconType="light">
    Ask for a fresh check every so often, monthly or yearly, to keep your population current without asking every time.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Re-verify every time" icon="shield-halved" iconType="light">
    Run a full liveness and uniqueness check on every visit, the highest bar, for payouts, labeling, or anything you cannot let slip.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Your rules are yours alone. A stricter setting on your platform does not change what any other platform does, and theirs does not change yours.

## Why a network is worth more than a list

On your own you can only recognize the people you have already seen. On the network, a person verified elsewhere is known to you on day one, and the value compounds as more platforms join. See [configuring your verification](/learn/configuring-your-verification) for the levers, and [what we store](/learn/what-we-store) for how we keep this private.
