[HumanCheck](/learn/what-humancheck-is) is a camera-based check that proves the person in front of it is a real, unique, live human, with no ID documents and next to no typing. Everything you run it with lives in a workspace: your checks, your team, your keys, and every result. Here is how to get one and what to have ready.

## What a workspace is

Your workspace is where everything HumanCheck does for you lives, at [platform.verifyyou.com/dashboard](https://platform.verifyyou.com/dashboard).

## How access works

HumanCheck workspaces are set up by our team. Talk to us and we will create your workspace and set you up with keys. You invite your own teammates once you are in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Talk to us">
    [Book a demo](https://sales.verifyyou.com) or get in touch however suits you. We will walk through what you are gating and what the check should do for you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="We create your workspace">
    We set it up around your use case and send an invite to your email. Your first check is configured with you, so it is ready for the job before you sign in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You sign in and invite your team">
    Follow the invite to the dashboard, then invite teammates from the Team page, as Member or Admin, so everyone who needs the results has them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to have ready

None of this is required to start the conversation, but having it ready means your check comes out right the first time.

- **Your company name.** It is shown to the person verifying, as in "Verifying you for [company]", so use the name your users know.
- **A redirect URL.** Where people land after they complete the check, usually the next page of your own flow. It is set on the verification itself.
- **A logo, if you want one.** Optional. Without it, the check shows a clean monogram built from the first letter of your company name. More on this in [branding your check](/learn/branding-your-check).
- **Your use case.** What you are gating (signups, a survey, a community, a marketplace) and roughly how strict you want to be. That shapes how we [configure your verification](/learn/configuring-your-verification) with you.

## What happens next

Once your workspace is live, the first session usually looks like this: you open the dashboard, find your check waiting, and run it yourself in test mode. Test keys run against a simulator, so the code path works end to end without a real person. When it looks right, the live key is the same integration with a different prefix.

Your check is configured for your use case from the start, and your VerifyYou contact tunes it with you as your needs change: stricter here, gentler there. The [quickstart](/learn/quickstart) takes you from that first test session to a confirmed real verification, with both a no-code path and a code path.

<Note>
  Wondering what to ask when we talk? The useful ones are: what should this check gate, how many verifications a month do you expect (pricing scales with your volume and use case, see [what it costs](/learn/pricing)), how strict should it be (see [security and friction](/learn/security-and-friction)), and do you want the no-code link or the SDK. Bring whichever you have answers for and we will work out the rest together.
</Note>
