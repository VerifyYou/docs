## The problem

Trust breaks at scale. The moment something is worth gaming, a signup, a survey, a payout, a gated offer, people find a way: bots, fake accounts, one person quietly running fifteen identities, bulk buyers. The usual fix is a full identity check. It works, but it is expensive, it asks your users to hand over documents and personal data you then have to hold and protect, and every step of that turns real people away before they finish. For most of these moments you do not need to know who someone is. You need to know they are real, and that one person cannot come back as fifteen. That is a much lighter thing to ask, so more real users make it through while the abuse still gets shut down.

AI has changed what a document is worth, too. A convincing fake ID, or a real face on a screen, is now cheap to generate, so asking someone for identity documents proves less than it used to and gets easier to spoof over time. What stays hard to fake is a real, live human who is present right now and cannot spin up a second account behind a second face. That live, unique signal is the one HumanCheck reads, which is why it holds up where a document check is starting to slip.

## The trust layer between no checks and KYC

HumanCheck is the trust layer between no checks at one end and full KYC at the other. Most teams reach for one of two other tools and find neither fits. Passive bot signals guess at risk but never prove a person, so a determined human still runs fifteen accounts. KYC proves a legal identity, but it brings the cost, the data, and the friction you were trying to avoid. HumanCheck sits in the gap: it proves a real, live, unique human without proving who they are.

Uniqueness is the part most teams are really after. You do not want your users' identities, and you do not want to hold their personal data. You want to know that when you block someone, it is hard for that person to come straight back. So uniqueness ties one real human to one account. One person cannot quietly run fifteen, and a blocked person cannot return under a fresh name. That is the whole pitch in one line.

## The economics of fraud

Fraud is an economic decision. Someone games a signup, a survey, or a payout when faking a presence costs less than what that presence is worth. For most of the internet's history that kept fraud in check, because passing as a real human took real effort. AI has erased that effort, and the cost of spinning up something that looks and behaves like a person is falling toward zero. The math tilts further toward the attacker every year.

HumanCheck changes it at the point of entry. When every actor has to be a unique, verified human before they get anything, the cost of creating a fake presence climbs sharply and the economics tilt back toward you. You stop cleaning up bad actors after the fact and make them uneconomic to create in the first place.

HumanCheck comes from people who fought this exact problem at internet scale. Marty Weiner, our CTO, was a founding engineer at Pinterest and Reddit's first CTO, where he spent years building systems to fight spam, bots, and abuse across hundreds of millions of users. He watched communities get overrun by fake accounts and ban evaders who spun up new profiles in minutes, and moderators burn out on the endless cleanup. ## What HumanCheck is

HumanCheck is a quick face scan a person does with their phone or their computer's webcam. No documents, no government ID, no wallet, just their face, in seconds. From that one scan it proves two things: liveness, that a real person is present right now and not a photo, a recording, or an AI generated face, and uniqueness, that this is one human tied to one account, so one person cannot quietly become fifteen and a blocked person cannot easily return under a new name.

[What a human check is](/learn/what-humancheck-is) explains both signals in plain terms, what each one proves, and how a returning person is recognized.

## How it works

A check is a short, hosted flow. The person does a quick face scan on our page, and the face becomes a face vector, a numerical representation of its geometry, never a saved image. Your server exchanges a token for a clear verified or failed result and gates access on that. You never receive a face.

When the person comes back, the policy is yours: a fresh check on every visit for the highest assurance, or quiet recognition of a returning guest by their device. [What a human check is](/learn/what-humancheck-is) walks the return moment step by step, and [how the check works](/learn/how-it-works) covers the full flow.
