Every verified person is deduped against a **uniqueness pool** — the set we check to guarantee one real human per account. v3 has two:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Account pool" icon="infinity">
    People who verify with a linked account (phone or [enrolled](/v3/verifications#enroll-a-user) identity). **Never cleared** — uniqueness is permanent, and a verified user's status travels with them across your integrations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Anonymous pool" icon="rotate">
    People who verify without an account (when `allow_anonymous` is on). **Resets monthly** — uniqueness is rolling, so an anonymous human can verify again after the reset.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Which pool a person lands in is decided by how they verify, not by a per-call setting. Turn on `allow_anonymous` in a verification's [config](/v3/verifications#config) to accept anonymous verifications at all.

<Note>
  Need anonymous uniqueness to last longer than a month — a season, a full
  event, a year? We can extend the anonymous pool's retention for your use case.
  Reach out.
</Note>

## Coming from v2 regions

Regions scoped a one-per-person limit to a context (a survey, an event). v3 splits that job in two:

- **Who's unique** is handled by the pools above — global per account, rolling for anonymous.
- **One action per human** is handled by [`consumable`](/v3/verifications#config) — mark the verification consumable and [consume](/v3/confirmations#consume-a-confirmation) the confirmation when the action's done.

Between them you get the same guarantees regions gave you, without managing region strings.
